HomoGenSE: Homomorphic Generative Speech Enhancement Online Supplement

Authors

Wenbin Jiang, Jingrui Qian, Yang Zhang, Qiang Zhao, and Kai Yu

Abstract

Most deep learning–based speech enhancement methods are discriminative and prioritize signal-level denoising, often at the expense of perceptual naturalness. Recent diffusion-based generative methods improve perceptual quality but typically incur high inference complexity and often struggle to preserve acoustic information. To address these limitations, we propose HomoGenSE, a hybrid homomorphic generative framework that balances signal-level quality, perceptual naturalness, and computational efficiency. Specifically, our method leverages the homomorphic signal processing paradigm to decompose the speech signal into excitation and vocal-tract components. The excitation dynamics are modeled by a generative module, while the vocal-tract components are estimated using a discriminative module. The two components are fused to synthesize the enhanced speech signal, and the entire model is trained end-to-end with a joint objective combining generative and discriminative losses. With a single function evaluation and only 5.3M parameters, HomoGenSE achieves high-fidelity speech enhancement. Experiments on two popular speech enhancement benchmarks demonstrate that HomoGenSE achieves competitive performance under diverse simulated and real-world conditions. Furthermore, speech recognition results on the out-of-domain LibriSpeech test set confirm the superiority of HomoGenSE in denoising while preserving acoustic information.
